After Banba completes his revenge, I'll greet him as I usually do when he gets home. That's my job.

Lin left without having Enokida find out Banba's whereabouts in the end. He took the escalator up to get to the ground floor and exited Hakata Station. It was still raining outside. Lin took out his umbrella and thought over everything as he walked back to the detective office where Banba was absent. Lin felt like he finally managed to piece the whole picture together from his conversation with Enokida. If he assumed Banba was trying to settle affairs from his past, then everything up until now made sense.

Banba had been living as a hitman in order to avenge his father. Once he accomplished his goal, he would no longer have a reason to continue hitman work. As such, he intended to go into retirement. When Genzo mentioned the reason was related to a motivation issue, he must have been alluding to this. Lin continued to walk down the street, dodging the puddles on the way. The streaks of pouring rain only grew. It was downpouring. Eventually, it had begun to thunder outside. Black clouds loomed over in the sky, making the area drewly dark as though it was night.

Lin had arrived to the building the office was in while he had been lost in thought.

"Ah."

He spoke unconsciously.

A car was parked out in front of the building. It was a red mini Cooper with white lines on its hood - it was Banba's vehicle. It had been a while since Lin had seen it here.

"He's already back?"

How anti-​climatic. Banba was not dying alongside the road somewhere. Lin had worried for nothing. Guess I didn't have to search for him anyway, he shrugged.

Just as Lin folded his umbrella and was going to ascend the stairs, he stilled. "…What is this?" Blood.

There was blood on the floor.

Fresh blood stains were leading up the staircase next to soddy footprints.

"Why the hell is there…?"

Lin muttered softly. What happened?

Lin followed the trial. He reached the second floor before proceeding to the third where the footprints and bloodstains were leading to.

It can't be. Lin's heart began to pound faster.

He felt a person's presence at the top of the stairs.

Someone's there.

Lin slowly ascended the stairs with caution, making sure not to make a sound, and peered over towards the office.

"Ah."

A man was there.

It was Banba. "…It was just you?" Lin sighed in relief.

Banba stood still in front of the door to the office. He must have noticed Lin's presence as he slowly turned around to face him.

The moment he did, Lin was greeted by an unimaginable scene.

"Wha-​"

Lin's eyes widened in shock.

Red liquid trickled off from Banba - specifically, blood.

"…No way, you're kidding."

It was not a spurt of blood that had gotten on him. A knife was plunged into Banba's stomach. Bright red blood thoroughly soaked his white clothes near his stomach. When Lin examined him closer, he noticed his arms were injured too. Red drops of blood were seeping out of his upper, left arm. There was a tear and what looked like to be a singe on his clothes. Had he been shot?

In the following second, Banba keeled over.

"H-​hey!"

Banba fell forward as though all the strength left his body at once, slumping against Lin.

"Banba!"

Lin went to support his body when he noticed: Banba was as cold as ice. He was thoroughly soaked from being pelted by the rain. But that was not all.

The warmth receded from his body due to the loss of blood.

"Hey, wake up! Banba!"

Banba did not respond no matter how loud Lin shouted. He was pale and his eyes were blank. He was barely conscious at this point.

"Shit!"

Lin had no idea what had happened, but at this rate it could be fatal. There were huge gashes on Banba. He was losing way too much blood. He probably needed a transfusion. An underground doctor would not suffice. Lin immediately called for an ambulance.

"Come on, you idiot. Don't you dare die on me." Lin clicked his tongue.

"Come on, wake up! Banba!"

Lin placed a hand against Banba's cool cheek and continued to shout his name.

After Enokida said his goodbyes to Lin at the Hakata Station, he went inside a nearby internet cafe. He shut himself in a non-​smoking booth and set up his laptop.

He got a new e-​mail in his personal account. When he opened it up, he saw the sender was Shigematsu. There was a short message which read, 'here's the fingerprints you asked for,' and the data of the comparison results were there as attachments. They were for the serial case of drugging-​robberies Lin was currently investigating.

The fingerprints showed they matched the ones found on the victims' belongings the police obtained. Shigematsu had gotten the help from another police detective and managed to have the fingerprints match with the man Lin had captured.

The fact the two samples matched meant the man Lin had captured was behind all the drugging-​robberies. Lin had requested Enokida to figure out the man's identity for him, so Enokida began to work right away. He laid out the culprit's belongings on the table: a wallet, a business card case, and three cell phones.

Enokida first looked through the man's wallet. The name Ryouta Ishikawa was listed on both the driver's license and insurance card, but no one existed under that name. The name, birth date, and family registry were all fake. There were a couple variations of business cards in the case. The titles on them varied from an attorney and judicial clerk to a security personnel and travel agent. He had used all these different identities to trick women up until this point.

"He's like a swindler." Enokida murmured with a smile.

The last items were the man's cell phones. Enokida hacked into the cell phone company's client database and found three different contractor names for each cell phone: Yasushi Uemu, Aiko Nakamura, and Kouki Bessho. The ages, sex, and addresses listed under each name were different. It was possible that these cell phones were purchased under borrowed names where no one could trace them. Other criminals do the same with their private devices.

But one of the names had caught Enokida's attention.

"Kouki, Bessho…?"

The other two contracts were fairly recent, but the device with the oldest contract had kept the same number since its beginning. The contract holder was a man named Kouki Bessho.

"Isn't this name-​"

Enokida thought he had seen that name before.

Naturally he would have as the name was listed in the data Nguyen had shown him in the business hotel. The man was Eitarou Bessho's little brother. Enokida began to quickly type on his keyboard. Once he sent a virus to someone's computer with administrative authority and hacked into it, he was able to infiltrate system to look up the family registry in the device. He extracted the information, and as he looked over Kouki Bessho's registry he had found Eitaro Bessho's name listed as well.

His investigation was complete.

Kouki Bessho was the robber Lin caught. Eitarou Bessho was the convict who murdered Banba's father. And coincidentally enough, the two were brothers.

"…I managed to hit the jackpot." Enokida was stunned.

He tried to call Lin to let him know, but the other did not answer. Lin could be on another call at the moment.

Guess I don't have a choice. I'll try sending it to him directly. Enokida printed out the information he found and typed on his computer as he gathered all the material. He was looking up Lin's whereabouts through his cell phone's GPS.

A red mark appeared on the map displayed on his screen. Lin's signal was coming from an emergency hospital within the city.

"…The ER?" Enokida cocked his head. "Why is he in a hospital?"

The ambulance had arrived only five minutes after Lin made the call. Banba was at death's door, unconscious and pulse weak.

The ambulance blared the siren and sped off to the emergency hospital in the city. Lin joined the ride to accompany Banba. The paramedics questioned Lin about Banba, but Lin was unable to given any information like his birth date, blood type and any ailments.

The red 'surgery in progress' sign lit up once Banba was rushed into the operating room on a stretcher. Banba had remained unresponsive even to the nurses during transportation as well.

Lin paced in front of the operating room restlessly. He kept moving around needlessly, unsure what to do and unable to keep himself still.

An hour passed, and yet Banba was still under surgery. It did not seem like the operation would be over anytime soon. It would be a long battle.

Just then‐ "Lin!"

Someone had called to him from behind. When Lin whipped around, he saw Shigematsu. The detective was pale as he ran towards him.

"Is Banba-​"

Sweat was visible on Shigematsu's brow. He must have sped here when he got Lin's memo. After catching his breath, he asked.

"…How's Banba doing?"

The tension in Lin eased now that someone he was familiar with was here. After letting out a sigh, Lin explained the situation. "The surgery is still going on. It sounds like he's in critical condition from what the doctors told me."

"What happened?"

"I don't know," Lin shook his head. "When I came back to the office, Banba was there, and he was covered in blood, and shortly after he lost consciousness…"

Lin had no idea how Banba had gotten in that state. But he knew for certain someone had attacked him.

"It looked like he had gunshot wounds. The hospital may report this to the police. That's why I thought about letting you know first."

"Yeah, I get it. I'll pass it along to the police, so don't worry about it."

Shigematsu nodded. "Where did Banba get shot?"

"His arm. But the more serious wound was from the knife. He got stabbed in the stomach, and there was so much blood."

Shigematsu's complexion changed the instant he heard those words. "…What type of knife was it?"

"It was just an ordinary survival knife. The handle was black. From its size, I'd say it was roughly ten centimeters long…But the blade was lodged into him up to the hilt, so I'm not certain."

Shigematsu's breath caught in his throat. He slumped into one of the chairs in the hallway and held his head. "I had no idea that would…"

Something was odd about his behavior. "Hey, what is it?" Lin gazed at Shigematsu.

"…I had a bad feeling about this." Shigematsu sighed and hung his head.

"…It's my fault." He muttered in a heavy voice. "If I stopped him, this wouldn't have-​…"

"Hold on. What do you mean?" What is he getting at? Lin frowned. "Do you know something about this?"

Shigematsu replied in a hoarse voice at Lin's demanding tone. "That knife is probably the same one I gave Banba."

Lin was not able to process the information. His brows knitted in confusion. Shigematsu fell silent, hands still bracing against his head.

"Shigematsu."

Lin sat down next to him and said with a stern expression.

"Tell me."

What had happened to Banba?

Even if Lin asked that question, he would still be unable to do anything. There was not a single thing Lin could do to help Banba other than to pray for him to come out of the surgery alive.

Nonetheless, Lin wanted to know.

"Please." Lin demanded. "Tell me everything you know about him." Shigematsu remained silent.

After the silence lingered for a couple beats more, Shigematsu stated in a near whisper. "…Don't tell Banba I told you this." He emphasized before beginning.

"Everything started from that one incident…It was the night I met him for the first time, as well as the night that tragedy happened."
