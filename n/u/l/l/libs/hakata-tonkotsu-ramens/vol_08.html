The driver dropped Banba off in front of the building. Once Banba got out, he turned around and spoke to the girl, "Misaki, I won't say anything to Jiro about this. I was an accomplice after all."

It was nearly past ten o'clock. If her parent learned he had made a junior work this late at night, he would get killed.

"But next time don't do nothin' on your own without tellin' Jiro, okay? You know how much he cares for you, right?"

"…Yeah."

Misaki hung her head. She seemed to be reflecting on her actions in earnest. "I'm sure Jiro will listen to whatever you want to say. Tell him your honest feelings."

Misaki nodded to Banba's lecture, "got it."

"Thank you for your help today, miss avenger."

Her eyes brightened up when he gently patted her head.

Without her help, he would not have achieved a single thing. She may be young, but she was someone he could rely on.

And Banba had one other helper this time as well. He turned to the young man in the driver's seat, "Méqué-​kun, thank you. You both helped me a ton." Méqué smiled creepily and waved from the driver's side. I should keep him a secret from Jiro too, Banba decided in his mind.

Banba watched the RV drive off into the distance before ascending the stairs to the third floor.

It was often said that baseball players who kept playing after getting injured would be found to have overexerted themselves after the match was over. They were distracted from the game, so the pain they felt was numbed. It was the same for Banba in this moment in time. The pain in his stomach was starting to come back full force now that the adrenaline his brain produced was receding. I overdid it, Banba thought to himself. He grit his teeth as he walked and finally made it to the Banba Detective Office.

The door was unlocked. Banba heard the sound of the TV when he got inside. He heard familiar terminology: 'bases loaded,' 'relieving the pitcher,' and 'pinch-​hitting.' A baseball game was on.

Lin was in the room. He was slurping a cup of ramen as he watched TV. He noticed Banba's presence and asked, still facing the screen, "is it finished?" "Yeah," Banba nodded. "It's over."

Even as he said that, he was not entirely sure himself. Could he really say it was over?

He had killed Bessho. Even so, he had not fully taken his revenge. Nothing regarding his situation had changed. He became a hitman in order to kill Bessho, and yet he felt miserable at the thought that his struggles over these past thirteen years had been unfruitful and meaningless.

"So what did you do with the body?"

Banba had promised to give Bessho's body to Lin, so he made sure to bring it back. "I left it at Saeki's place. You can do whatever you want with it." "Okay," Lin said before finally turning to face him. He examined his face and frowned.

"So why did you come back here?"

"What?"

"Go back to the hospital."

"…Oh. I forgot," Banba whispered.

He had completely forgot that Lin told him to go the hospital after he was done. His mind was blank, and Méqué went ahead and brought him back here. Banba had unconsciously brought himself here, just like back then. "I don't know how it happened. I just ended up here before I realized it." "What the hell's that?" Lin snorted. "Well, whatever. Shigematsu will show up here soon enough. He'll bring you to the hospital."

If Lin had stayed here, then Banba's cell phone had to be in this office right now. Shigematsu would come here in a matter of time, believing Lin to be Banba. But Banba did not have any need to run from him anymore. He could just sit tight and wait to be caught.

"…Oh, that reminds me. Here, Lin-​chan." Banba took out the bloodied knife and handed it to him. It was the knife pistol Lin had lent him. "Thank you for doin' so much for me."

Lin had truly saved him, not just with the cell phone problem. Had he not shown up, Bessho may have killed him.

"It was nothing really," Lin expressed indifferently before moving his attention back to the TV.

It seemed the Hawks were losing. The score was six to three. They were three points behind, but they had two outs and bases loaded. They had a window of opportunity.

In the following moment, there was an uproar. The live announcer shouted excitedly. Someone had managed to hit a home run. The audience all stood up and cheered. Some of the fans were caught on camera, crying. The person who had came from behind with a grand slam was none other than the veteran player who had announced his retirement later this year.

"…Oh right. That reminds me, Banba."

Banba heard Lin's calm voice as the announcer shouted in glee.

"Welcome back."

Banba's breath caught in his throat. He felt like that one phrase had saved him in some way.

Thirteen years. It had been a long time. He had lost many things up to now, but he also gained other things in return. There were people he would not have met had he not taken this path.

He felt like there was meaning and value to this after all.

"…I'm home."

It was bustling with activity on the TV screen. The player ran around the diamond and returned to the benches, where his teammates crowded around him and showered him with praise. A former professional baseball commenter regrettably spoke on how incredible it was for him to send the ball flying in the opposing direction of its course and how the player could still play for another couple years.

But even if they take leave from active duty, life did not end.

The player who had hit the home run answered to his supporters' calls and took off his hat with a bow. His broad back with his number on it seemed to be exclaiming loud and clear through the TV screen: I'm not done yet.

There's more to come.

Banba sat down next to Lin, pushing back against the pain in his stomach, and lightly smiled.

He felt the urge to cry a little.

Hero Interview

Nakasu City was busy on a Friday night. It felt like there was more people passing over the Fukuhaku Deai Bridge than usual.

Banba abruptly stopped as he walked over the bridge. There was a crowd forming in the bridge's center. The people were getting excited. When Banba went to see what was happening, he saw several people crowd around a clown in red. The clown was magnificently juggling three clubs with great skill.

Banba met eyes with the clown. In that moment a bright grin formed on the clown's painted white face. Banba returned a smile and approached him. There were coins and bills left in the hat left on the ground at his feet. The clown seemed to be making a good amount of money tonight. Banba went and put in a 10,000 bill. The clown bowed respectfully.

Banba withdrew from the crowd and went to stand in the center of the bridge, gazing at the night scenery of Nakasu. The memory from thirteen years ago would always resurface whenever he came here: the day he spotted Masataka and had chased after him. Everything had started in this very spot. I'm sure being quite sentimental, he reflected to himself.

After a couple minutes passed, the person he was waiting on had arrived. Banba saw Shigematsu wearing a worn out suit. He raised his hand as he ran over to him from the other side of the bridge. Shigematsu walked over to

Banba and asked, "are your injuries better now?"

Banba nodded as he placed a hand to his stomach. His wound still hurt, but it had mostly healed. "I wouldn't have showed up if it didn't."

A lot had happened over the past week. Banba had visited his father's grave, crossed the sea to see his teacher, and met with his father's murderer. He had fallen into critical condition, in a coma, and teetering on the line of life and death. He caused everyone on the Ramens to worry.

"You went too far…I seriously thought you wouldn't make it this time," Shigematsu sighed.

"I gave you a lot of headaches." "Don't do anything like that ever again." He lightly shoved at Banba's shoulder. "I'll treat you for a beer as an apology."

Banba had asked Shigematsu to come out here. He had to return the favor for getting him one piece of the evidence from the case.

However Shigematsu declined, "No thanks. I'll treat you today to celebrate your recovery."

If that was the case, Banba will gladly accept the offer. He expressed his thanks.

Shigematsu abruptly asked him, as though he had just remembered: "More importantly, what are you going to do from now on?"

Banba tilted his head slightly, unsure what he meant. "What do you mean by what I'll do?"

"You're going to quit being a hitman, right?"

"Oh," Banba voiced in understanding. That was right; he did mention that.

He had decided he would quit after this last kill.

"No," he shook his head, withdrawing his earlier statement. "I'm not quittin' yet."

"…What?"

Shigematsu exclaimed entirely dumbfounded.

"But didn't you say you'd quit after your next kill?"

Banba did indeed say that. He was going to quit this job after taking revenge for his father by killing Bessho. That was the plan.

However, an unexpected development had occurred.

Banba smiled wryly. "I did plan on it, but I no longer want to."

"How come?"

Shigematsu had cocked his head, not following.

"I talked with Bessho," Banba broke the ice. He thought back to the last conversation he had with Bessho. "Bessho told me the truth of what happened thirteen years ago."

"This is everything I know."

Bessho remained unwavering and unafraid after he had finished his explanation. It did not seem like he was making any of it up.

The person Banba had loathed over these past thirteen years was nothing more than a hitman for hire. This man had merely followed his orders. He killed his father for money - not to make his family suffer. Banba felt dejected upon realizing this truth.

However‐

"…But that don't change the fact you killed my dad."

The man had done what was asked. He was just doing his job. But those could not be used as excuses. He could not fully condemn the client for all the injustice. Having the title of a hitman would not excuse their crimes. "That is true," Bessho agreed, his expression calm. "I'm well aware of that. It's true that I killed him. I knew this day would come eventually. Someone would judge me someday. Punishment for what I did was a given. That is the fate of a hitman." Bessho smiled.

"So go on. Kill me." His eyes were determined.

Banba had a sudden thought. Someone would judge him. Punishment was certain. Would there be a day that will happen to him too? Would he be able to stand up tall like Bessho was right now when that day came?

It was a strange feeling. Even though Banba was facing the person he wanted to kill more than anything, he did not feel anything in this very moment. Even his resentment had faded. He knew nothing would change after he killed this man. He could even let him go and it would be the same. Regardless, he had to kill this man. It was not for his father's sake - it was for his past self from thirteen years ago, for his current self over these past thirteen years, and to take responsibility.

At Bessho's prompting, Banba readjusted his grip on the knife pistol. He pressed the tip of the blade against Bessho's heart. He then mustered up the strength to push it in.

Warm, thick blood spurt out. Bessho whimpered softly, eyes widening. Truthfully, Banba intended to stab him in the stomach just like he did to his father. He had considered making him suffer and die slowly. But he did not do that. He pierced Bessho right in the heart, allowing him to die almost instantly. His moments of suffering were cut short.

This was his way of giving thanks.

Banba looked down at Bessho's body and whispered quietly, "…Thanks to you, I found my resolve."

The resolve to plummet into the pits of Hell.

He reached a hand towards Bessho's lifeless face and gently closed his eyelids.

There were a few things he came to know because of Bessho."Someone had apparently hired him. He killed my dad for good pay."

And there was more sides to the matter as well. The murderer who had taken the life of his loved one and whom Banba had resented over these thirteen years was not truly wicked at heart. He was a mere hitman, no different than himself.

Shigematsu was stunned by what Banba told him. "Hired by whom?"

Banba shook his head. "I still don't know yet."

The one answer to a question he wanted to know the most was left unanswered. But he did have a clue. Bessho told him that the male client was one of Banba's blood relatives. At least he speculated so, but a hitman's intuition was something that should not be ignored.

Regardless, it was certain there was a mastermind behind the incident thirteen years ago. There was a reason why the Banba household got targeted. And he had to find out the truth behind it.

To achieve this, he needed to remain in the underground industry. "There's a no good-​doer who I can't let live in this world." Banba spoke softly in a near whisper as he gazed into the night, "I ain't quittin' until I kill him."

"I see." Shigematsu nodded minutely. "Come on. Don't look so serious." He wrapped an arm around Banba's shoulder and smiled. "We're going to

celebrate your recovery. Let's have fun drinking tonight." Banba took up Shigematsu's suggestion. "Might as well."

For today only, he will put everything in the back of his mind and indulge in drinking. They will go bar hopping, and he will come home bright and early in the morning and get yelled at by his roommate for reeking of alcohol. And this was fine - at least for today.

Banba walked towards Nakasu city, shoulder to shoulder with Shigematsu.
